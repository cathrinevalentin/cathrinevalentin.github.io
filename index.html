Profile

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Political and Social Sciences at the European University Institute (EUI) in Florence. My research interests center on digital spaces. In my work, I examine the political implications of an increasingly online public debate, including its impact on shaping political preferences and behavior.

My work is interdisciplinary, and I study these questions using a combination of quantitative methods across multiple data sources. In some projects, I conduct large-scale data collection from social media and computational text analysis; in others, I carry out qualitative analyses of argumentation in citizens’ online engagement with misinformation. I am currently conducting an online survey experiment on the political influence of online in-groups, recruiting respondents via social media ads and simulating the group-level consequences of these dynamics in an agent-based model.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I worked as a research assistant at the Centre for Social Data Science (SODAS) at the University of Copenhagen, and I contribute to the HOPE Project .

I am on the 2025–2026 academic job market. If any of this sounds interesting to you, feel free to reach out.
